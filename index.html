About Me

More About Me

I have been thankful for the resources at freeCodeCamp.org. The path that they offer to introduce a newbie to coding is the most welcoming and encouraging that I have found. I think that the gradual introduction to how a static website works is a great way to get people started. I found the JavaScript course to be especially edifying; this was where I felt I had really entered in to programming and could start to work with the logic of computers.

I've found my knowledge of Excel to be a great resource for programming. I have experience in creating spreadsheets and workbooks that function as small applications for my colleagues. These would allow them to get insight on key performance markers with as little data entry as possible. This came by the strategic use of tables and formulas allowing for limited duplication of data.

In addition to my affinity for technology and tools, I am an avid outdoorsman and family man. I spend as much time as possible with my family. Whether it's going on our small fishing boat or playing at the park, I appreciate every moment that we spend together. I also volunteer as the archery coach for the school where my wife teaches.
